Shahi Paneer

Description

It is a classical North Indian rich and creamy curry recipe prepared with a combination of dry fruits with onion sauce and paneer cubes. In Shahi Paneer, the word ‘Shahi’ literally means royal in Hindi, and the reference is attached mainly because of its rich and luxurious profile. It is also believed that the recipe and the gravy sauce were first introduced during the Mughal empires and served to the royal family. Hence, it carries the name and serves it on auspicious and religious occasions. A simple and creamy Shahi Paneer Masala Recipe is prepared in many ways. The most common way is to make it with a combination of nuts like almonds, cashews, and walnuts and diced onions. The tomato is skipped as it does not yield a rich colour, however in some variation, even tomatoes are added. Adding tomatoes will change their colour and texture, and it is typically added in dhaba-style recipes. Further, the curry can also be used as a multi-purpose sauce and other vegetables and meat tenders can be added to the same sauce.

Ingredients

  • For Onion Tomato Puree
    • 1 tbsp Butter
    • 2 pods cardamom
    • 1 inch cinnamon
    • 1 pod black cardamom
    • 3 cloves
    • 1onino, sliced
    • 3 clove garlic
    • 2 tomato,chopped
    • 1 cup water
    • 1 tbsp salt
  • For Curry
    • 1 tbsp butter
    • 1/2 tbsp shahi jeera
    • 1 bay leaf
    • 1/4 tbsp turmeric
    • 1 tbsp kashmiri red chilli powder
    • 15 cubes cottage cheese
    • 1/4 cup cream
    • few threads saffron
    • 1/4 kasuri methi,crushed
    • 1/4 tbsp garam masala

Steps

  1. firstly, in a kadai heat 1 tbsp butter and saute 2 pods cardamom, 1 inch cinnamon,1 pod black cardamom and 3 cloves.
  2. Now add 1 onion, 3 cloves garlic and 1 inch ginger.
  3. Saute slightly until onion softens.
  4. further, add 2 tomato and saute slightly.
  5. Also, add 1 cup water and 1 tsp salt. mix well.
  6. Cover and boil for 20 minutes or until it softens completely.
  7. Cool completely and transfer to a blender. blend to smooth paste without adding any water.
  8. Sieve the puree making sure the puree is smooth and silky. keep aside.
  9. In a large kadai heat 1 tbsp butter and saute ½ tsp shahi jeera and 1 bay leaf.
  10. Keeping flame on low add ¼ tsp turmeric and 1 tsp chilli powder.
  11. Saute slightly until the spices turn aromatic. Add in the prepared tomato-onion puree and mix well.
  12. Further, add ¼ cup cream and mix until everything is well combined.
  13. Add in 15 cubes paneer, few threads saffron and mix well.
  14. Cover and simmer for 5 minutes or until flavours are absorbed well.
  15. Now add ½ tsp kasuri methi and ¼ tsp garam masala. mix well.
  16. Finally, enjoy shahi paneer with roti or garlic naan.
